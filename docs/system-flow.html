Sales Production Procurement / inbound Dispatch Decision
The two loops that connect it

Buy → store → make: when a job needs a part that isn't in the store, that shortfall raises a PO → the goods arrive (GRN) → pass inward QC → land in stock → and the waiting job can finally reserve them. That arrow back from the store to the component check is the whole point.

Rework: a failed QA gate re-opens itself, so a batch is re-inspected before it can ever close.

Step by step

The happy path in eight moves

  1. Price the product on its Cost Sheet; that price flows into a Quotation.
  2. Confirm the quote → Sales Order. The system checks stock (net requirements).
  3. If short, a Manufacturing Order is raised — plus a nested sub-assembly MO for the motor.
  4. Components in store are reserved (FIFO); anything short triggers a Purchase Order.
  5. The PO is confirmed → a Goods Receipt (GRN) appears → Inward Quality accepts or rejects the lot.
  6. Accepted goods land in the store (FIFO) and become available to the waiting job.
  7. The job builds and must pass the mandatory QA Gates before it can close.
  8. Finished goods move to stock and are delivered to the customer.
