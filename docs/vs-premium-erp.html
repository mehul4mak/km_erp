The principle

What "done the right way" means here

Beyond features, a few architectural choices are simply correct — and worth naming, because they’re why the foundation is solid even though the breadth is young:

✓ Right

Controls enforced in the engine

QA and inward-QC gates are server-side rules — they can’t be bypassed from the UI or an API. Many ERP rollouts leave these as optional process. Ours can’t be skipped.

✓ Right

One source of truth

Parts, quantities, costs and stock live in one connected model. No parallel spreadsheets to drift.

✓ Right

Documents that link themselves

Auto-numbered references with an Origin trail give real traceability from order to receipt, for free.

✓ Right

Complexity hidden, not removed

The full engine is underneath; the UI shows only the customer’s real workflow. Power without the clutter.

▲ Gap

Finance isn’t wired in

Operations are correct, but they don’t yet post to accounts. For a full ERP that loop has to close.

▲ Gap

Governance is thin

Roles, approvals, audit and real auth need to be built before this is trusted with many users.

Bottom line

The honest verdict

Where we stand

The foundation is done right — real manufacturing engine, enforced controls, connected data, correct costing. On the core make-buy-stock loop we’re genuinely on par with premium, because we stand on the same proven engine — and on enforced quality and inward controls we’re arguably ahead of a default premium install.

Premium ERP wins decisively on breadth: integrated finance, traceability, planning, governance and scale. Those are real, and we’ve named them — not hidden them.

For this customer — a focused manufacturer with five concrete pain points — KMForge is the better fit and the better value today. It is a sharp, correct subset, not a replacement for enterprise ERP at scale. The roadmap (finance, traceability, roles) is exactly the path from "excellent fit" to "full platform."
