The problem we set out to fix

From our discovery session, the way product costs live in the business today:

What we heard

  • 1Cost sheets live in Excel and go stale. A component rate changes at the vendor, but the sheet keeps quoting the old number until someone remembers to edit it.
  • 2The motor is costed by hand. You build the motor in-house from a dozen parts, so its cost has to be worked out separately and typed back into every mixer's sheet.
  • 3No single source of truth. The real structure — which parts, how many, at what rate — sits across files and in people's heads, so two sheets rarely agree.
